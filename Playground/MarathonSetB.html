Marathon Product Set

Enhancing the discovery and purchase of curated product sets to increase cross-selling and customer confidence.

Role: UX Research Intern
Timeline: June–August 2025

Initial Research Focus

The Problem

Marathon runners often face decision fatigue and time constraints when shopping for gear. We needed to test if curated marathon outfits could simplify the experience, increase trust, and drive higher apparel sales.

The Goal

How might we offer product sets that are easily accessible and match user needs—specifically for marathoners?

What We Learned

Comfort is King

Runners valued comfort over everything—avoiding irritation, blisters, and sizing issues.

Customization is Crucial

Users wanted options to swap items (e.g., shorts to tights) and customize colors.

Visuals Drive Confidence

Virtual mannequin suggestions were frequent – users wanted to “see” outfits visually before buying.

Trust Outweighs Price

Trust in the brand and gear functionality often outweighed cost concerns for serious runners.
