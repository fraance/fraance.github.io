Service Design

Small Wins, Big Changes

October 2023 - February 2024

Project Background

Catch22 partnered with the Royal College of Art (RCA) in October 2023 to create an alternative impact tool measuring the distance traveled for prison leavers. The project aimed to capture and quantify the progress of Service Users in the Personal Wellbeing Service, co-creating a meaningful assessment tool that moves beyond traditional scoring methods.

Team

This project was a collaborative effort with:

  • Alomi Parikh (Ex-Product Designer)
  • Aishwarya Joshi (Ex-Product Designer)
  • Douwe Kruyt (Google Analyst)

Guided by tutor Judah Armani, a multi-award-winning designer in social impact, with expertise in criminal justice and homelessness.

My Role

As an equal member of the design team, I contributed to co-designing the theory of change and co-creating an Excel-based tool alongside Catch22 wellbeing practitioners. My responsibilities included gathering insights from practitioners and service users, identifying key challenges, and validating our design hypotheses to ensure the toolâ€™s relevance and effectiveness.

Learnings

This project deepened my understanding of designing for social impact in complex environments. I learned the value of small, measurable progress markers for individuals on probation and the importance of trust-building through tools that genuinely capture their journey. This experience underscored the need for adaptable solutions that resonate with both service users and practitioners, ensuring meaningful data collection that benefits all stakeholders.
